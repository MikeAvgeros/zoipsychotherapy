Practice Policies

1. Data Protection & Privacy Notice (UK GDPR)

To provide safe, ethical, and effective psychodynamic psychotherapy, Synthesis Practice needs to collect and process personal data. We operate strictly under the following lawful bases outlined in the UK GDPR:

Lawful Bases for Processing:
Article 6(1)(b) Contract: Processing is required to fulfill our therapeutic contract with you.
Article 9(2)(h) Health or Social Care: We process 'Special Category Data' (mental and physical health details) to deliver professional therapeutic treatment.

Information We Collect

  • Personal Data: Full name, date of birth, home address, phone number, email address, and emergency GP contact details.
  • Special Category Data: Brief, anonymized clinical session notes, psychological history, relevant medical backgrounds, and medication formulations.

Data Storage & Protection Metrics

Digital records and contact histories are stored within a fully GDPR-compliant practice management software platform protected via Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). System controls implement practitioner-level privacy blocks, preventing therapists from accessing each other's records during standard operations. Physical forms or handwritten entries are anonymized using a unique client code and kept in locked filing cabinets completely separate from your identifiable data.

Data Retention Limits

In accordance with professional indemnity insurance requirements, all clinical records are retained securely for a period of 7 years following the formal termination of therapy, after which they are permanently and securely destroyed.

Your Legal Rights

Under the UK GDPR, you hold the right to issue a Subject Access Request (SAR) to access data we hold on you, request corrections to factual inaccuracies, or lodge formal complaints directly with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).

2. Clinical Continuity & Peer-to-Peer Coverage

Synthesis Practice is owned and operated by four practicing mental health professionals. This policy ensures that if your individual therapist experiences sudden incapacitation (acute illness, severe injury, or death), the remaining co-directors can seamlessly step in to protect your welfare, manage crisis pathways, and maintain clinical care boundaries without breaching data laws. In specific configurations, your clinical supervisor may act as your Clinical Trustee to ensure matching clinical approach continuity.

Emergency Data Access Protocol

Every therapist operates within a strict "need-to-know" data silo; no director has routine access to the active client registries or clinical notes of the other three therapists. This configuration is bypassed only upon official emergency notification. Upon activation, your appointed Primary Clinical Trustee is granted access strictly to your coordinator's active calendar and contact registry solely to inform you of the disruption and facilitate onward care paths. Raw psychodynamic process notes remain restricted unless required for immediate risk management.

3. Therapeutic Cancellation & Attendance Framework

In depth psychodynamic psychotherapy, the consistency of the day, time, and physical or virtual space forms an essential part of the therapeutic "holding environment". A reliably recurring space allows deep, unconscious processes to surface and be examined safely. Missing or cancelling sessions disrupts this clinical frame and directly impacts the therapeutic process.

Standardized Fee Terms

The Fixed-Slot Model: In individual therapy, clients are allocated a specific, recurring weekly time slot reserved exclusively for them. Because this slot cannot be filled on short notice, sessions cancelled or missed by the client are charged at the full standard rate, regardless of the notice window or reason given.
Alternative Notice Frameworks: Where specific contract arrangements apply, cancellations made with less than 7 days’ notice will incur the full session fee.

Rescheduling & Absences

If a cancellation is planned with significant notice (e.g., an unavoidable medical procedure), your therapist will make reasonable clinical attempts to offer an alternative time slot within the same working week, subject to room and practitioner availability. Clients are never charged for sessions cancelled by the therapist or if the company is closed for public holidays. Therapists will provide a minimum of 4 weeks' notice for any planned annual leave, which typically aligns around Christmas, Easter, and the summer.

4. Therapeutic Endings & Post-Treatment Policy

The ending phase is one of the most critical parts of psychotherapy. A planned closure helps you consolidate and sustain positive therapeutic changes. Because therapy activates intense internal dynamics, you may experience a sudden urge to terminate or "drop out" during challenging periods. To protect your progress, clients commit to the following closing framework:

  • Prohibition of Abrupt Exit: You agree not to terminate therapy abruptly via email, text message, or voicemail without discussing the decision within a clinical session.
  • Mandatory Review Sessions: If you feel the desire to complete your treatment, you commit to bringing this directly into a session to explicitly explore whether the urge stems from a therapeutic plateau, defense mechanisms, or true readiness.

Required Notice Layout Periods

Short-Term Therapy (< 6 months): A minimum of 2 sessions dedicated exclusively to processing the ending frame.
Long-Term or Open-Ended Therapy (> 6 months): A structured ending period of 2 to 4 months is required to safely unpack and integrate the deeper relational dynamics established during the work.

5. Safeguarding & Risk Management Policy

Where a client presents with active risk elements, the practice operates under a collaborative risk-management framework integrating with public healthcare systems where required. If risk escalates to a point where a client’s life or the life of a third party (specifically children or vulnerable adults) is in imminent danger, confidentiality must be breached to ensure safety.

Where clinically viable and safe, your individual clinician will explicitly inform you that confidentiality must be breached. Only the minimum data required to secure safety will be shared directly with your GP or local crisis stabilization teams.

6. Subject Access Request Procedures

Clients can make a SAR verbally or in writing to any Director. Upon receipt, we must verify your identity using official documentation to prevent unauthorized data exposure. Under UK GDPR rules, data will be provided without delay and at the latest within one calendar month of receipt. No fee is charged for standard requests.

Any identifying information relating to third parties mentioned in session notes will be completely redacted. Given the sensitive nature of raw psychodynamic unconscious process records, if the treating clinician believes disclosure would cause serious mental or physical harm, professional indemnity legal advice will be sought before data is withheld.

7. Complaints Procedure

If you are dissatisfied with any aspect of our service, we encourage you to raise this directly within a session with your individual therapist. Working safely on relational ruptures and reparations is often highly valuable to your clinical growth.

If an informal route is unsuccessful, you may lodge a formal complaint in writing via email. To guarantee complete impartiality, if the complaint is against a specific director, an uninvolved co-director will be appointed to review the case. We acknowledge formal complaints within 5 working days and provide a full written response within 28 days. You retain the right to escalate your concern directly to your therapist's independent professional governing body (BPC, UKCP, or BACP).

8. Financial Controls, Invoicing & Arrears

To maintain transparent financial trails, payment is accepted strictly via direct electronic bank transfer (BACS) or secure digital links (Stripe/SumUp). We explicitly do not accept cash payments or anonymous checks.

  • Assessment Phase: Operates on a pre-payment model. All initial consultation fees must be settled and cleared a minimum of 24 hours before your arranged session takes place.
  • Ongoing Therapy: Invoices are issued at the end of each calendar month and are due strictly within 7 days of the invoice date.
  • Arrears Policy: If an invoice remains outstanding past 14 days without an explicit, clinically authorized payment plan, the practice reserves the right to temporarily suspend further therapy sessions until arrears are cleared.

Annual Fee Modifications

Synthesis Practice reviews its operational fee metrics annually during September to account for inflation and clinic room space indices. Existing clients receive a minimum of 2 months' notification both verbally in session and via written notice before any adjustment takes effect, allowing ample time to process the logistics clinically.
