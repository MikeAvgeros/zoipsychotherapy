Clinical Modalities

Dance Movement Therapy

Dance Movement Psychotherapy is suitable for children, adolescents, and adults and can support individuals experiencing anxiety, trauma, emotional difficulties, identity exploration, relationship challenges, neurodiversity, and mental health concerns. No previous dance experience is required, as the emphasis is on authentic expression rather than performance or dance technique.

Dance Movement Psychotherapy (DMP) is a body-based psychotherapeutic modality that uses movement, non-verbal communication, and creative processes to support emotional, psychological, and relational well-being. It is based on the principle that mind and body are interconnected and that movement reflects patterns of thinking, feeling, and relating to others.

Through a safe and therapeutic relationship, clients are invited to explore emotions, experiences, and aspects of themselves through movement, body awareness, symbolic expression, and verbal reflection when appropriate. The process aims to foster self-awareness, emotional regulation, resilience, and personal growth.

Therapy Format

Sessions

60-minute sessions are held on the same day and at the same time each week, providing consistency, stability, and a secure therapeutic space.

Frequency

Sessions are usually held once a week. In some cases, more frequent sessions may be beneficial depending on your needs, goals, and the nature of the therapeutic work.

Location

I offer therapy primarily online via Zoom, providing flexibility and accessibility wherever you are. For online sessions, you will need a private and confidential space where you feel comfortable to speak freely and, where appropriate, move without interruption.

Age group

Adults aged 18 and over.

Languages

Therapy is available in English and Greek.

Length of Therapy

Dance Movement Psychotherapy is a depth-oriented approache that goes beyond symptom management. Together, we explore the underlying relational patterns, emotional experiences, beliefs, and life events that shape how you relate to yourself and others. Therapy focuses not only on what is happening in your life, but also on understanding the why and how behind your experiences, allowing for meaningful and lasting change. There is no fixed length of therapy. This is generally a long-term therapeutic approach, as meaningful change often takes time and unfolds in layers. The duration of therapy is tailored to each individual and depends on your needs, goals, and personal journey. We will regularly review the work together to ensure it continues to meet your needs.

Getting Started

Taking the first step towards therapy can feel daunting. It is completely natural to wonder whether therapy is the right choice for you or whether I am the right therapist to support you.

To help you make that decision, I offer a free 30-minute introductory phone or video consultation. This is an opportunity for us to discuss what has brought you to therapy, explore what you are hoping to gain from the process, answer any questions you may have, and see whether working together feels like the right fit. There is no obligation to continue afterwards.

If you decide to take the next step, we will arrange a 60-minute initial assessment session. During this meeting, we will explore your current concerns, personal history, and therapeutic goals in greater depth. The assessment helps us identify the issues that may require further exploration and support, allowing us to develop a shared understanding of your needs and how Dance Movement Psychotherapy can best support you.

If we decide to continue working together, we will agree on a regular weekly appointment and discuss the therapeutic agreement, ensuring that the therapeutic process is collaborative, safe, and tailored to your individual needs and pace.
