Clinical Modalities

Systemic Family Therapy

Offering a space to explore relationships, communication, and patterns of interaction, helping children, young people, couples, and families navigate challenges and strengthen connections.

Systemic Family Therapy is a ‘talking therapy’. It is called “Family Therapy” because it is often for people in families. It is called “Systemic Therapy” because it is also for people in other kinds of groups, relationships or ‘systems’. Systemic therapists help family members share their views and feel heard and understood. Therapists are skilled in managing the kind of differences of opinion and stressful feelings, which can sometimes be produced by family discussions. They also help family members look for solutions to their difficulties; this might mean sharing their ideas with family members as well as helping them find their own answers. In addition, therapists help families to think about their strengths and encourage them to recognise what they are still managing in life, despite the difficulties.

Ultimately, this approach avoids placing blame on any single individual. Instead, it views challenges as something shared, focusing on how the whole system can work together to create lasting, positive change. By fostering deeper empathy and collaboration, systemic therapy empowers you to build long-term resilience and find healthier, more connected ways of moving forward together.

Meet the therapist

Areas of Clinical Focus

01

Relationships & Connection

Healthy relationships are central to emotional wellbeing. Systemic Therapy provides a space to strengthen connections, deepen understanding and foster more fulfilling relationships with yourself and others.

02

Life Transitions & Change

Major life events such as loss, parenthood, separation, the arrival of a new baby, career changes or moving country can feel overwhelming. Therapy can help you make sense of these transitions, adapt to change and move forward with greater confidence.

03

Family Patterns & Identity

Our relationships, family experiences and wider social contexts shape how we see ourselves and others. Together, we explore these patterns to better understand your experiences and create opportunities for meaningful and lasting change.

Therapy Format

Sessions

60-minute sessions, held at the same day and time each week.

Frequency

Usually the duration of treatment is between 6-12 sessions, but in some cases it might last longer.

Location

Online therapy via secure video conferencing platforms, allowing sessions to take place from a comfortable and convenient setting.

Age group

Children, young people and adults.

Languages

Therapy is available in English and Greek.

Length of Therapy

Systemic Therapy can be short-term and longer-term. During our initial assessment, we can think together about what may be most appropriate for you.

Getting Started

Taking the first step towards therapy can feel like a big decision. To help you decide whether Systemic Therapy and my approach is right for you, I offer a free 15-minute initial conversation. This is an opportunity to ask any questions you may have, discuss what has brought you to therapy, and learn more about how I work.

If we decide that working together feels like a good fit, the next step is a 90-minute initial assessment. The first meeting is a chance to be clear about what the difficulties are and whether the therapy might be helpful. We usually ask you to invite family members or other people supporting you to the first meeting.
