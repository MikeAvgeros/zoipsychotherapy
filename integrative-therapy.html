Clinical Modalities

Integrative Therapy

Integrative Psychotherapy is a flexible, evidence-informed approach that recognises that every person is unique. Rather than relying on a single therapeutic model, it brings together techniques and theories from different evidence-based approaches to create therapy that is tailored to your individual needs, experiences, and goals.

At the heart of integrative psychotherapy is the belief that emotional wellbeing is influenced by the connection between our thoughts, emotions, body, relationships, past experiences, and current life circumstances. By exploring these areas together, therapy can help you better understand yourself, develop healthier coping strategies, strengthen resilience, and create lasting change. therapists in this modality work collaboratively with clients in a safe, compassionate, and non-judgemental environment. Depending on client's needs, therapy may draw on approaches such as person-centred, psychodynamic, cognitive-behavioural, attachment-based, mindfulness-informed, and body-oriented interventions.

Integrative psychotherapy can support people experiencing:

Anxiety, stress, and panic

Depression and low mood

Trauma and post-traumatic stress

Relationship and family difficulties

Domestic and sexual abuse

Low self-esteem and body image concerns

Grief and loss

Life transitions and identity issues

Emotional regulation and resilience

Personal growth and self-discovery

Getting Started

Therapy begins with an assessment where we explore your concerns, personal history, strengths, and goals. Together, we create a personalised treatment plan that evolves as your needs change.

Sessions are collaborative, confidential, and paced according to what feels safe and appropriate for you. Our aim is not simply to reduce symptoms, but to help you build greater self-awareness, emotional wellbeing, healthier relationships, and a more fulfilling life
